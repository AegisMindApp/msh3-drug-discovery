Subject: EHDN Seed Fund Application — MSH3 ATPase Drug Discovery for Huntington's Disease

Dear Flaviano,

I am writing to express my interest in submitting a research proposal to the EHDN Seed Fund program for Phase 0 binding assays validating FDA-approved drug candidates targeting MSH3 ATPase. This work is coordinated with CHDI (Cure Huntington's Disease Initiative) and aligns with their drug discovery research priorities.

BACKGROUND:
MSH3 overexpression drives somatic CAG repeat expansion in HD striatum. Recent genetic evidence (Moss et al. 2017, Flower et al. 2019) establishes MSH3 modulation as disease-modifying, yet no small-molecule inhibitors have reached clinical development. This represents a critical therapeutic opportunity.

RESEARCH SUMMARY:
Through multi-method computational screening, I have identified FDA-approved drug candidates targeting the MSH3 ATPase domain:

1. **EPTIFIBATIDE** — GP IIb/IIIa antagonist (FDA-approved for acute coronary syndrome)
   - Three-way convergent hit (selected by docking, Nash equilibrium game theory, and multi-target Bayesian optimization)
   - Nash synergy score: 0.2503 with laquinimod
   - Rapid repositioning pathway (GMP manufacturing established, extensive safety database)

2. **PONATINIB** — BCR-ABL/VEGFR kinase inhibitor
   - Highest MSH3 docking affinity (pKd 6.36)
   - CNS-compliant pharmacokinetics
   - Approved clinical use (CML)

3. **ENTRECTINIB** — TRK/ROS1/ALK kinase inhibitor
   - MSH3 pKd 6.03
   - Excellent BBB penetration
   - Approved for oncology

All computational results (1,759 docking scores, surrogate model ρ = 0.854, Nash synergy matrices, multi-target BO data) are publicly available at: https://github.com/AegisMindApp/msh3-drug-discovery

PROPOSED PHASE 0 STUDIES (Months 1–3):
1. Recombinant MSH3 binding kinetics (SPR, ITC, HTRF) for EPTIFIBATIDE, PONATINIB, ENTRECTINIB
2. Dose-response curves and Kd determination
3. Secondary screen: off-target activity profiling

EXPECTED OUTCOME:
Identification of lead compound(s) with sub-micromolar MSH3 affinity, enabling rapid advancement to cell-based CAG repeat expansion assays and preclinical PK/PD studies.
